The Angelo Rock

The Stone of Anjuro. A famous landmark. It was created when an unknown highschooler pummeled both a rock and Anjuro Katagiri at the same time and integrated them together somehow. Despite its scary and grim looking appearance, it is surprisingly familiar with the townspeople and has gained popularity as a meeting point for lovers. People say they can hear the rock growl "Agios" at times.

Directions: Take the bus in Morioh, go towards the direction of Jōzenji road. Get off at the third stop and walk for one minute.

Boing Boing Cape

A fisherman started the rumor when he saw a young woman falling from the cliff. It was assumed to be a girl who was trying to commit suicide by jumping off the cliff but she miraculously bounced back up. The local fishermen all pray for good luck to this particular rock, calling it a 'sacred spot'. Many also believe that there is a god of the sea at work. If one asks any local fisherman where 'Boing-Boing Cape' is, they'll gladly point it out.

Ghost Girl's Alley

Ghost Girl's Alley is a hidden road in the town of Morioh that is believed to be the purgatory between life and death. Residents have claimed to have seen 'Reimi Sugimoto', a girl who was murdered years ago, down the street. The road is a part of the Town's mythology at this point, you cannot find it yourself, people who have seen it claim that it dissapears after you're done with it. All that is truly known about it is that the street 'loops' as you walk around it until you are 'allowed' to leave. But those who claimed to have spoken with Reimi Sugimoto, say that she pressured them to never look behind them as they travelled the loop... or something would happen. But it's still unconfirmable if the street exists or not, as this is the only available photo and it doesn't match up with the town layout. Perhaps you might find it if you decide to come?

Trattoria Trussardi

Trattoria Trussardi is a recently established Italian restaurant owned and managed by Tonio Trussardi. It is next to Kunimitōge Graveyard. He is the only employee there, the restaurant only features two tables, each having around 2-3 chairs. Trussardi's is unique in that customers do not choose their meals. Instead of a menu, Tonio will simply look the customer down and determine their meal based on certain problems pertaining to the customer's health. From this, he is able to create certain foods, to appeal to each of the customer's problems. The restaurant uses only the finest Italian ingredients in its meals.

Super Fly

On an abandoned telephone tower lives a man by the alias of Toyohiro Kanedaichi. He claims to have bought the metal tower 3 years ago for $100,000 and has not touched the ground since. He lives a life of complete self-sufficiency, but is missing things such as salt and sweets. If given food and spices, he'll let people take pictures with him. A rumour started circulating that the tower cannot let someone leave unless another steps in to take their place, explaining why Toyohiro doesn't come down. It is unknown how the nickname 'Super Fly' came about for the tower, but it likely has something to do with the rumour.

Rohan Kishibe's House

Residence of famous manga artist: Rohan Kishibe.

Total Area: 3.4 Acres (340 SQ. M)

The house features a living room, dining room, kitchen and attic, plus seven other rooms. The house was rebuilt due to a fire with repair costs exceeding 7 million yen.

Directions: Don't bother. He'll pretend to be out anyways.
